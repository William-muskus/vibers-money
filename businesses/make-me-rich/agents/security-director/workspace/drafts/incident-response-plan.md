## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose
This Incident Response Plan (IRP) establishes the framework for responding to security incidents within make-me-rich. The plan defines roles, responsibilities, and procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

### 1.2 Scope
This plan applies to all security incidents affecting make-me-rich systems, data, or operations. It covers:
- Cybersecurity incidents
- Data breaches
- System compromises
- Malicious attacks
- Security policy violations

### 1.3 Objectives
- Minimize the impact of security incidents
- Ensure timely detection and response
- Preserve evidence for investigation
- Restore normal operations as quickly as possible
- Prevent recurrence of incidents
- Maintain compliance with regulations

## 2. Incident Response Team

### 2.1 Team Structure
- **Incident Response Manager**: Oversees the entire incident response process
- **Technical Lead**: Leads technical investigation and containment
- **Communications Lead**: Manages internal and external communications
- **Legal/Compliance**: Ensures compliance with legal and regulatory requirements
- **HR**: Manages personnel-related incidents
- **Subject Matter Experts**: Provide specialized knowledge as needed

### 2.2 Roles and Responsibilities

#### Incident Response Manager
- Overall coordination of incident response
- Escalation management
- Decision-making authority
- Ensures compliance with IRP

#### Technical Lead
- Technical investigation of incidents
- Containment and eradication
- System recovery
- Forensic analysis

#### Communications Lead
- Internal communications
- External communications (customers, partners, regulators)
- Public relations
- Media relations

#### Legal/Compliance
- Legal implications assessment
- Regulatory reporting
- Documentation for legal proceedings
- Compliance with data protection laws

## 3. Incident Classification

### 3.1 Incident Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Response Time | Escalation |
|-------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| **Critical** | System compromise, data breach, major service disruption | Immediate (0-1 hour) | CEO, Board, Regulators |
| **High** | Significant security event, potential data exposure | Within 4 hours | Management Team |
| **Medium** | Security event with limited impact | Within 24 hours | Department Head |
| **Low** | Minor security event, no immediate impact | Within 72 hours | Technical Team |

### 3.2 Incident Types
- **Unauthorized Access**: Attempted or successful unauthorized access
- **Malware Infection**: Detection of malware or ransomware
- **Data Breach**: Unauthorized disclosure or access to sensitive data
- **Denial of Service**: Service disruption attacks
- **Insider Threat**: Suspected malicious activity by insiders
- **Physical Security**: Physical security breaches
- **Policy Violation**: Violation of security policies

## 4. Incident Response Process

### 4.1 Preparation

#### Incident Response Plan Maintenance
- Regular review and updates to IRP
- Training and exercises
- Resource allocation
- Tool and technology readiness

#### Training and Awareness
- Regular security awareness training
- Incident response training for team members
- Tabletop exercises and simulations
- Documentation and procedures review

### 4.2 Detection and Reporting

#### Detection Methods
- Monitoring and alerting systems
- Log analysis
- Anomaly detection
- User reporting
- Third-party notifications

#### Reporting Procedures
- **Initial Report**: Report to Incident Response Manager
- **Incident Form**: Complete incident report form
- **Escalation**: Escalate based on severity
- **Documentation**: Document all details of the incident

### 4.3 Containment

#### Short-Term Containment
- Isolate affected systems
- Disable compromised accounts
- Block malicious IPs
- Implement temporary fixes

#### Long-Term Containment
- Remove malware
- Patch vulnerabilities
- Restore from clean backups
- Implement permanent fixes

### 4.4 Eradication

#### Root Cause Analysis
- Identify attack vector
- Determine cause of incident
- Document findings

#### System Remediation
- Remove malicious code
- Patch vulnerabilities
- Restore systems to known good state
- Implement compensating controls

### 4.5 Recovery

#### System Restoration
- Restore from backups
- Verify system integrity
- Test systems before returning to production
- Monitor for recurrence

#### Business Continuity
- Restore business operations
- Minimize downtime
- Coordinate with business units
- Communicate restoration status

### 4.6 Post-Incident Activities

#### Lessons Learned
- Conduct post-incident review
- Document lessons learned
- Identify improvements
- Update procedures and policies

#### Incident Report
- Prepare comprehensive incident report
- Include timeline, impact, response actions
- Document root cause and remediation
- Submit to management and stakeholders

#### Follow-Up
- Implement recommended improvements
- Conduct follow-up reviews
- Update training materials
- Monitor for similar incidents

## 5. Communication Plan

### 5.1 Internal Communication
- **Incident Response Team**: Regular updates during incident
- **Management**: Status updates based on severity
- **Employees**: Notification of incidents affecting them
- **Documentation**: Maintain incident log

### 5.2 External Communication
- **Customers**: Notification of incidents affecting them
- **Partners**: Notification of incidents affecting partnerships
- **Regulators**: Compliance with reporting requirements
- **Media**: Public statements as needed

### 5.3 Communication Guidelines
- Be transparent but protect sensitive information
- Provide accurate and timely information
- Coordinate messages to ensure consistency
- Designate spokesperson for public communications

## 6. Monitoring and Metrics

### 6.1 Key Performance Indicators
- **Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)**: Average time to detect incidents
- **Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)**: Average time to respond to incidents
- **Incident Resolution Time**: Time from detection to resolution
- **Incident Frequency**: Number of incidents over time
- **Incident Severity**: Distribution of incident severity levels

### 6.2 Reporting
- Regular incident reports to management
- Quarterly security metrics review
- Annual incident response effectiveness review
- Compliance reporting as required
