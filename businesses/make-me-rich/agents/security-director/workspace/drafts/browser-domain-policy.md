# Browser Domain Access Policy

## Version: 1.0
## Effective Date: [Date]
## Last Reviewed: [Date]

## 1. Policy Statement

### 1.1 Purpose
This policy defines the requirements for browser domain access within make-me-rich. It establishes the framework for requesting, approving, and managing access to external domains to ensure security and compliance.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all agents and systems within make-me-rich that require access to external browser domains for any business purpose.

### 1.3 Objectives
- Ensure secure access to external domains
- Minimize security risks from browser-based activities
- Maintain compliance with security policies and regulations
- Provide clear guidelines for domain access
- Document all domain access decisions

## 2. Domain Access Requirements

### 2.1 Approved Domains
- All browser domain access must be approved by the Security Director
- Access to unapproved domains is strictly prohibited
- Approved domains are listed in the Approved Domains Registry

### 2.2 Request Process
- All domain access requests must follow the Domain Access Request Process
- Requests must include complete justification and security assessment
- Approval is required before any domain access is granted

### 2.3 Security Controls
- Implement browser security extensions for all approved domains
- Use sandboxed environments for high-risk domains
- Apply content filtering and blocking as needed
- Monitor all browser activity for suspicious behavior

## 3. Domain Risk Classification

### 3.1 Risk Levels

| Risk Level | Description | Access Requirements |
|------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Low** | Well-known, reputable domains (e.g., google.com, microsoft.com) | Standard approval |
| **Medium** | Moderate risk domains with known vulnerabilities | Standard approval with mitigations |
| **High** | High risk domains with security concerns | Escalated approval (CEO) |
| **Critical** | Malicious domains, phishing sites, blacklisted domains | Access denied |

### 3.2 Risk Assessment Factors
- Domain reputation and history
- SSL/TLS certificate validity
- Blacklist status
- Known security vulnerabilities
- Content and purpose of domain
- Compliance with security standards

## 4. Approval Process

### 4.1 Standard Approval
- Request submitted to Security Director
- Security risk assessment conducted
- Business justification reviewed
- Approval decision within 48 hours

### 4.2 Escalated Approval
- High-risk domains require CEO approval
- Security Director conducts initial assessment
- CEO makes final approval decision
- Decision within 72 hours

### 4.3 Denial Process
- Immediate denial for critical risk domains
- Denial reason provided to requesting agent
- Alternative solutions suggested if available
- Documentation of denial decision

## 5. Domain Management

### 5.1 Approved Domains Registry
- Maintain centralized registry of all approved domains
- Include domain, approval date, expiration, and conditions
- Regular review and updates
- Accessible to all agents

### 5.2 Domain Review Cycle
- Quarterly review of all approved domains
- Reassessment of risk levels
- Removal of unused domains
- Updates based on new information

### 5.3 Domain Removal
- Immediate removal of domains with new security risks
- Removal of domains no longer needed
- Removal of domains violating policies
- Documentation of removal decisions

## 6. Security Monitoring

### 6.1 Activity Monitoring
- Monitor all browser activity for suspicious behavior
- Log all domain access attempts
- Alert on unauthorized access attempts
- Regular security audits of domain access

### 6.2 Incident Response
- Immediate investigation of security incidents
- Containment and eradication procedures
- Documentation of incidents
- Post-incident review and improvements

## 7. Training and Awareness

### 7.1 Agent Training
- Training on domain access policies
- Security awareness for browser usage
- Recognition of phishing and malicious sites
- Reporting procedures for suspicious activity

### 7.2 Regular Updates
- Quarterly security awareness sessions
- Updates on new threats and risks
- Review of domain access policies
- Case studies of security incidents

## 8. Compliance and Auditing

### 8.1 Compliance Requirements
- Compliance with security policies
- Compliance with regulatory requirements
- Compliance with industry standards

### 8.2 Auditing
- Regular audits of domain access
- Review of approval processes
- Verification of compliance
- Documentation of audit findings

## 9. Policy Compliance

### 9.1 Compliance Measurement
- Regular measurement of compliance with this policy
- Reporting of compliance metrics to management
- Corrective actions for non-compliance

### 9.2 Exceptions
- Exceptions to this policy must be approved by the Security Director
- Exceptions documented with rationale
- Exceptions reviewed quarterly

### 9.3 Non-Compliance
- Violations of this policy may result in:
  - Revocation of domain access
  - Disciplinary action
  - Legal action if applicable
